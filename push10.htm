Quick2Cloud College - Course 205

Add Push Notifications to Android Mobile Devices

 

Things are about to get a level more exciting now that you have decided to communicate with your mobile devices from your Quick2Cloud Sample Application in the IBM Cloud.  You are entering a new phase of software development, no longer confining your coding solely to the Cloud but extending it to your hand-held devices for a real personalized experience.  This is going to be a lot of fun!

 

But, before you get started, a little information needs to be conveyed on what is to be accomplished in this course.  Several more pieces of software are required to integrate with mobile systems but that will be no problem, Quick2Cloud will go over each piece and assist you in the knowledge required.  You will be using:

 

1.       Google’s Firebase system

2.       Google’s Android Studio

3.       Google’s Android Studio SDK

4.       IBM Push Notification Service

5.       IBM Push Notification Software

6.       Gradle build system

7.       Android Device Emulator

8.       IBM Auth Server

9.       Your personal Android mobile device

 

But don’t worry, Quick2Cloud will take each piece and assist you into getting it installed, initialized and configured to make push notification a reality.  And, it will do so in conjunction with all work done prior with your Quick2Cloud Sample Application.  If you don’t have a Quick2Coud Sample Application, you may want to take course 100 and get one.

 

Let’s look at what you will be accomplishing.  But first a comment. The notification process is somewhat simple once you understand all that is required for success; however, its achieving that understanding, that is the hard part and without Quick2Cloud’s assistance it would take some intense amount of research to get it working.  So, let’s have a look at how Quick2Cloud can make IBM Push Notification, for your Android devices, fairly easy.

 

Your sample application will construct and issue an Android Push Notification to your mobile device by using the IBM Cloud’s Push Notification Service.  Each time someone accesses your sample application, you will send a notification to your personal Android device indicating that that activity took place.  To accomplish this, the following structure of components and software must be in place in the configuration shown below.

 

 

You will start by creating a Google Firebase account (1).  This is accomplished by registering at Firebase, creating an userID and password.  Firebase is Google’s gateway in supplying Android devices push notifications and you must go through this Google Service for that purpose.  Next, you will install Google’s Android Studio and SDK (2) that will allow you to develop, build and run your Android Push Notification App on an Android Emulator (5) as well as the Android physical device such as your mobile phone (6).  Following Android Studio’s install, you will create an IBM Push Notification Service (3) from the IBM Service Catalog, which will supply your sample application with push credentials (7) and allows for the downloading (4) of a sample Push Notification program suitable for running in the Android Studio emulator and on your physical Android device (6).

 

After interacting with IBM’s Push Notification Service and Google’s Firebase solution, between them you will be assigned five different security keys and/or IDs.   The secret to making the interconnections above a valid path for notifications is the proper configuration of all the pieces.  Any mistake in those configurations and notification will not work!  Therefore, Quick2Cloud will assist you at every numbered area above to get components created and configured correctly.  At the end of the assists, you will be ready to issue Push Notifications to your Android devices.

 

Step 0

 

Firebase Services (1)

 

Let’s begin by creating an account with Google’s Firebase Service located at www.firebase.com.  Here you will define your Android Application Package; you will name the programs you will be using for IBM’s Push Notification Service and they will be the names of the ones IBM provides in their Android App example.  Later, when you gain an understanding and have push notification working, you will be able to edit the Android App to suite your needs or create one from scratch.   But for now, stick with the IBM names in this course.

 

On the Firebase home page select the Sign in link as shown below.  

 

www.firebase.com

 

 

Step 1

 

Create an account and supply your email address and create a password.  Once you have accomplished this, click on the Go to Console link in the above image next to Sign in.

 

Click on Add Project as shown below.  You will do this for every Android project you design now and in the future.  For now, you will have only one and that is the one for this course.

 

 

You will use the name q2cproject when prompted.  This name will signify that this is the Quick2Cloud project and will be differentiated from future projects that you create.  Then click on Continue.

 

 

Take the default of using Google Analytics as shown below.  You don’t have to enable Google Analytics, in fact, in your course you won’t be using them.  However, it doesn’t hurt to turn them on in case you wish to enable analytics later (a parameter in the Gradle Build file to be shown later). 

Click on Continue.

 

 

Select the default Google Analytics account from the drop-down box then click Create Project.

 

 

At this point Google will provision you the resources for this project which will take about 30 seconds.  Once completed, proceed by clicking on the Continue button.

 

Step 2

 

Fantastic!  You have a Firebase Project provisioned and have almost completed the first step in doing mobile push notification.  Next, you will define your IBM Push Notification Android Applications to your Project.  You will be using the following names provided by IBM in their sample Android Application so do not change them.  (You will download the IBM Sample Android Application shortly).

 

On your Firebase display you should see the following icons:

 

 

For this course you will be selecting the Android Icon. 

 

Note:  There will be another course on setting push notifications to IOS devices but for now this course focuses on Android only.  Click on the Android Icon.

 

Use com.ibm.mobilefirstplatform.clientsdk.android.push as the Android Package Name.  Use IBM Android App as the nickname then click on Register app.  Again, do not change the package name in this course or push notifications will not work.

 

 

Step 3

 

Now, you will register one more application from IBM in the same fashion as above.  To accomplish this, you want to find the Add Application button again.  To find the button, use this technique:

 

At far left on the menu bar, click on the Project Overview settings icon and select Settings.

 

 

Scroll down the display until you see the add app button.   Click on Add app.

 

 

Select the Android Application Icon then fill in the application name for this second application. 

 

 

Use com.ibm.mysampleapp as the Android Package Name.  Use My Personal push notification app as the nickname.  Then click on Register app.

 

Step 4

 

You now have the two Android Applications registered and now what you need is the configuration file that Google Firebase creates for your development as you proceed.  For now, you will save this file until later, so remember where you put it.  However, if you forget where you stored it you can always come back to this display and download again.

 

Click on the Download google-services.json link.

 

 

Inside the google-services.json are the configuration definitions that Android Studio will use to integrate in your mobile application.  When you get to the next few steps you will be moving this file over to your Android Source Code directory.  Until then, let it sit where you saved it.

 

Step 5
