Add Push Notifications to Android Mobile Devices

 

Enabling Push Notifications on your physical Android Device

 

You are at the last step; it’s been a long journey in configuration, but you made it.  Let’s take one more look at the configuration diagram and see what was accomplished.  Everything in gray has been completed, tested and proven successful.  Only the green box at the bottom of the diagram is left to configure.

 

 

You will now enable your Android Physical Device to receive push notifications from your Quick2Cloud Sample Web Application.  Grab your smart phone, a USB cable and your enthusiasm because the end is very much in sight.

 

Step 0

 

When you plug your Android Device into your laptop, in this case a smart phone, your device will appear as an external memory disk exposing your mobile device’s files, photos and videos.  When you view your available mobile device from Android Studio, all you will see are your mobile device emulators as shown below with your physical device nowhere in sight.

 

 

The procedure outlined below is to get your physical android device to show up as an available device in the list above.  Once it shows up as available, by selecting it, you can run your IBM Supplied Android App on your physical device instead of the emulators.

 

Step 1

 

Note: The steps below may be different for the various Android operating systems and network vendors.  However, the concepts are the same; it’s just the navigation to accomplishing them may have a slight variant.

 

To accomplish this slightly tricky procedure, you must change some settings on your Android device and put it in Developer mode.  Go into settings and navigate to About Phone.  Click on the About Phone link.

 

 

Step 2

 

Click on the Software information link

 

 

Step 3

 

Click on the Build number a total of 7 times.  At first it will seem like nothing is happening but, on the 7th click, a small window will pop-up saying you are now in Developer Mode.

 

 

Step 4

 

Next, you will go back out to Settings and navigate to Developer options.  Click on Developer options.

 

 

Step 5

 

Turn on USB debugging by moving the slider to on.

 

Note:  If this is the 2nd thru nth time you are connecting your mobile device to Android Studio, you may need to disable USB debugging first, then reenable it or Android Studio may not recognize the mobile device and display unknown as the device name.

 

 

Step 6

 

Go back to Android Studio and you should now see your physical mobile device as one of the options in the list.

 

 

Run MainActivity again from the Run pull-down making sure your physical device name has been selected as shown above.  Look at your mobile device and you should see the IBM Supplied Android Application window open, complete with an app icon in your device’s application folder.  Remove the USB Cable from the physical device and you are good to go.

 

Note: You will want to revoke the USB debugging setting after the app is running for security reasons.

 

Android Mobile Device

 

 

Congratulations!  You did it, you have your Quick2Cloud Web Application pushing notifications to your physical mobile device.  Any other Android device you connect can also get the push notification app and when your web application pushes a notification, then all devices you connected will receive the messages as well.  Spend some time, modify the IBM Supplied Android App and make it your own.
