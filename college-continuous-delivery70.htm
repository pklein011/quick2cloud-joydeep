Quick2Cloud College - Course 104

 

Implementing Continuous Delivery for your Cloud developed sample application

 

Quick2Cloud wants to make the final step into using toolchains and Continuous Delivery a progressive step forward encompassing your work in Course 100 & 101 around applications and Service provisioning.  However, one thing you will quickly learn about Toolchains is that by default, they are not direct descendants of that work, rather, uses that work as a reference, requiring that work to be re-constituted specifically for the chain’s use. 

 

For example, Toolchains don’t automatically adopt knowledge about your Services bindings or your URL name that was created in your earlier college course works.  With the help of Quick2Cloud and its assistance, that information prior learned will be re-introduced to the Toolchains as part of its Pipeline Deploy Script.

 

Let’s take a look at that Delivery Deploy Script to better understand why:

 

Step 0

 

Login to the IBM Cloud.  On the drop-down on the left side of the IBM Dashboard click on DevOps.  You will see a list of your Toolchains.  Pick the Production Toolchain and click on the Pipeline Deploy icon in yellow below.

 

 

Step 1

 

Build and Deploy are the two pieces that make up Pipeline Deploy.  Click on the settings icon on Deploy and then click on

Configure stages

 

 

Step 2

 

Once the configuration opens, scroll down toward the bottom to see the current Deploy Script being used to deploy your sample application.  Notice, by scrolling the script, that there is no mention of your IBM Services or URL anywhere.  But, not to worry, with some simple hand holding from Quick2Cloud we will add them in to get back to parity with your prior college course work.

 

 

The needed Deploy Script for your sample application is shown below.  All lines that start with yellow must be added to the existing script in the relative position shown.  You must replace the ibmID with your real IBMID where specified. 

 

What this will do for your Toolchain’s Deployment:

 

1.      Bind your Cloudant Service

2.      Add URL for your application

3.      Restart your application

 

Below, Pipeline Deploy script with added Quick2Cloud changes in yellow

 

 

Tip: Don’t forget to replace the ibmID with your real IBMID, the one you use to login to the Cloud. 

 

In this example, ibmID-qvDB would be the same name you used when you created the Service back in Couse 101. 

 

If you used a different name then put in that different name instead by replacing ibmID-qvDB with your Service name.

 

Here are the commands needed to update the deploy script as shown above

 

This assumes your Service name is

ibmID-qvDB

 

your URL is

ibmID-qvsample.mybluemix.net

 

Substitute and then Cut & Paste each line into their proper script location

 

cf bind-service $CF_APP ibmID-qvDB

cf create-route $CF_SPACE mybluemix.net --hostname ibmID-qvsample

cf map-route $CF_APP mybluemix.net -- hostname ibmID-qvsample

cf restage $CF_APP

 

Once these changes are in place on both the Production and Development Toolchains then you have most everything completed to run with Continuous Delivery.  To prove that these edits are correct you will instruct the Build piece of Pipeline Deploy to restart your application so these changes can take place.

 

Click on the yellow icon below under the Build tile and this will start the entire build process over again and run your newly updated deploy script.  This time, when the Toolchain deploys your application, it will be bound to your Service and be addressable by the URL you provided.  This build process takes several minutes to finish.

 

 

Note:  Your new Production URL is https://ibmID-qvsample.mybluemix.net

 

If Build or Deploy shows an error, look into their logs (via link on their tile) and see what went wrong.  Correct the error, which should be easy since it is very explicit to the problem and press the above button to restart the build once again.

 

Important:  In the IBM Cloud, when restarting your second Toolchain, it may fail due to an over quota condition (see Deploy log). 

 

Reason: If it does, it is because when you restart a Toolchain it does not stop the existing one in favor for the new one.  IBM Cloud starts an additional Toolchain that is really your restarted Toolchain and waits for that additional Toolchain to initialize before it stops the Toolchain it is replacing.  That is how it accomplishes non-disruption to the Customers.  At the last second it switches all IP:Port requests to the additional Toolchain then stops the old one.  Because of this, there is a point in time where all three Toolchains are running - if only for a moment.  During that moment you may be over quota and IBM Cloud will fail the additional Toolchain before it is initialized.  To solve this problem if it should occur, stop the old Toolchain first by going to the IBM Cloud Dashboard and selecting Stop from the three-dot drop-down to the far right of the Toolchain name.  Then go to the DevOps display, click the Pipeline Deploy tile on the Toolchain being restarted and click on the Deploy Play Button icon.  That will restart the Build and Deploy – Build will say Stage Running.

 

When you upgrade your IBM Account to a Pay-as-you-go this Toolchain problem will go away.

 

You are now set up for Continuous Delivery.  Use Eclipse to edit your sample application on the Development Toolchain, make all the changes you want and when you commit and push those changes then Delivery Pipeline will automatically restart your Development Toolchain sample application with no disruption to your development Customers. 

 

When you are ready to go production then you will use the procedure coming up to get your Development Toolchain code to the Production Toolchain Repository, again, causing no disruption when your new Production version is restarted.
